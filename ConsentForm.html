Finger Dataset Collection Consent Form

This task is part of a research study conducted by Jeffrey Bigham at Carnegie Mellon University and is funded by Carnegie Mellon University.

The purpose of the research is to make everyday interfaces more accessible to people with visual impairment. The dataset collected will be used to train a model that detects users' finger locations and will be publically available.

Procedures

If you participate, you will be ask to take and upload 5 pictures, including 4 pictures of your index finger in front of some appliance, and 1 picture of the same background without your finger.

The duration of these tasks will be at least one minute and no more than one hour.

Participant Requirements

Participation in this study is limited to individuals aged 18 and older.

Risks

The risks and discomfort associated with participation in this study are no greater than those ordinarily encountered in daily life or during other online activities. As with other tasks in your daily life, you may tire or become bored completing our tasks. There is also a risk of confidentiality being breached.

Benefits

There may be no personal benefit from your participation in the study but the knowledge received may be of value to humanity.

Compensation & Costs

You will be compensated for your participation in this study with $10. There will be no cost to you if you participate in this study.

Confidentiality

The images you upload will be included in a publically available dataset. To protect your privacy, you will be asked to upload images that do not contain any personally identifiable information, and any image of including personal identifiable information will be removed from the data set.
